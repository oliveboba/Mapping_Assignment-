Limited English Proficiency Across the New York City Boroughs Speaking Spanish

By: Olivia Sippel


NEW YORK, New York City is one of the most diverse cities in the world, that has many different spoken languages. Next to English, the second largest spoken language is Spanish.

Each borough represents a different amount of Spanish speakers, specifically those that are not fluent in the language. When someone living in New York City requires an interpreter for city, state or federal services, they are considered "limited english proficient".

Out of each borough, The Bronx has the most Spanish speakers, while queens comes in second. While this map only represents one language, there are many different languages spoken in each borough.

Three surrounding community districts each have different amounts of food pantries. These three areas attempt to help each other out.


New York City requires all city agencies to improve access to city services for all New Yorkers. This ensures that commonly distributed documents are translated into multiple langauges as well as have a telephonic interpretation for at least 100 languages. This gives better access to limited English proficient individuals residing in in the city, including the second most spoken language.
